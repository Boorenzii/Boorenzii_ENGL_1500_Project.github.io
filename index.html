My Software Engineering Challenges

Imposter Syndrome

During my first year at Iowa State, I often questioned whether I belonged in software engineering. Surrounded by peers who seemed to grasp concepts effortlessly, I grappled with self-doubt. However, through persistence and support from mentors in the Computer Science department, I learned that everyone faces similar doubts, and these feelings are part of the growth process.

Technical Hurdles

The transition from basic programming to complex data structures in COMS 2280 tested my resilience. Each debugging session in the Coover Hall labs, though frustrating, taught me valuable lessons about patience and analytical thinking.

Time Management

Balancing coursework with my role on Iowa State's varsity esports team required learning crucial time management skills. Creating the team's analysis program while maintaining academic excellence taught me how to prioritize tasks and work efficiently under pressure.

"The biggest challenge wasn't learning to code – it was learning to believe in myself and embrace the process of growth. Through the support of the ISU community, I discovered that every obstacle was an opportunity to learn."

How I Overcame Challenges

I found strength in connecting with fellow students during SI sessions and the Software Engineering Club. Realizing I wasn't alone in my struggles made a huge difference. Through tutoring and collaborative projects, I began to recognize my own growth.

Growth Mindset

Learning to see mistakes as learning opportunities rather than failures changed my perspective. Each error in my code became a chance to deepen my understanding. This mindset shift was crucial for my development as a software engineer.
